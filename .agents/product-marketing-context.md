# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-22*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Logan POS is an offline-first point of sale system for East African shops that need sales, stock, credit, and profit in one place.

**What it does:** Logan POS helps retailers sell faster, track inventory, manage customer credit, record running costs, and understand daily profit. It is built for unreliable internet, so cashier work can continue offline and sync when the connection returns.

**Product category:** Point of sale, retail management software, inventory management, small business software.

**Product type:** SaaS web app / PWA with backend API.

**Business model:** Subscription plans for small and growing businesses, with setup/support services from Logan Tech.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Shops, minimarkets, wholesalers, pharmacies, electronics stores, cosmetics stores, hardware stores, and multi-branch retail businesses in Tanzania, Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, and nearby markets.

**Decision-makers:** Owner, manager, accountant, branch supervisor.

**Primary use case:** Replace notebook/manual sales tracking with a reliable POS that works online and offline.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Sell products quickly and keep stock accurate.
- Know profit, expenses, and credit balances every day.
- Keep customer and business records safe when internet is unreliable.

**Use cases:**
- Cashier selling from a phone, tablet, or laptop.
- Owner checking sales, stock, credit, and profit reports.
- Manager adding products, stock batches, prices, and running costs.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Small retailers lose money because sales, stock, credit, and expenses are tracked in separate books or memory.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Many POS tools depend too much on stable internet.
- Some systems show revenue but not net profit.
- Credit sales are hard to track by customer.
- Stock added offline can disappear or fail to sync clearly.

**What it costs them:** Lost stock, unpaid customer debt, wrong buying decisions, slow cashier service, and no clear profit view.

**Emotional tension:** Owners want control but do not want a complicated enterprise system.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** POS systems such as Kyte, Loyverse, Odoo POS, and local reseller POS tools.

**Secondary:** Excel, WhatsApp notes, paper receipt books, and notebooks.

**Indirect:** Doing nothing because "the shop is still working."

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Offline-first cashier workflow.
- Profit report with product cost and running costs.
- Customer credit balance visibility.
- Stock batch tracking and low-stock alerts.
- East Africa payment context: M-Pesa, Azampay, mobile money workflows.

**How we do it differently:** Logan POS combines the daily cashier workflow with the owner dashboard. It focuses on practical shop realities: unstable internet, credit customers, mobile money, and stock control.

**Why that's better:** The owner gets usable control without needing a separate accountant or complex ERP.

**Why customers choose us:** Simple enough for a cashier, useful enough for an owner, and local enough for East African retail habits.

**Anti-persona:** Very large enterprises that need deep ERP customization before using a POS.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Manual records are slow, unreliable, and do not show profit.

**Pull:** One system for sales, stock, credit, expenses, and reports.

**Habit:** Owners are used to books, Excel, and WhatsApp notes.

**Anxiety:** Fear that staff will not learn it, internet will fail, or data will disappear.
